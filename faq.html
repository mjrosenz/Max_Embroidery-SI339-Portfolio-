faq

how can i start embroidering?

Just like a friend of mine told me, all you need is your dear friend YouTube and some motivation. Originally, I sought out people I knew who embroidered already, but they all told me the same thing. It is truly one of those hands-on hobbies that require practice to learn.

how long does it take?

For an average design, it takes anywhere from three to five hours. I like to spread out my time when I make my designs. I’ll try to do a little more each day.

what supplies do you need to embroider?

You need a needle, needle threader (One of the most underrated, essential tools,) embroidery floss, embroidery hoop, scissors, pencil, and embroidery fabric. Sometimes, I like to use a seam ripper when I make big mistakes.

what's your favorite part of embroidery?

My favorite part has to the feeling of accomplishment when I finish a portion of a design. Seeing how each little thread comes together on the fabric can be so euphoric.

where do you get your supplies?

I got a starter kit from Amazon that was about $12. I wouldn’t spend more than $20 on your first set of supplies. Then, once I knew I wanted to keep embroidering, I visited my local Joann Fabrics and got a nicer hoop, some transfer paper for designs, and extra embroidery floss.

what size embroidery hoop do you use?

Currently, I have been using a 6-inch hoop. I think that anything smaller than that is going to be a waste of time.

can anyone embroider?

Honestly, no. It requires a lot, a lot of patience as well as a steady hand. Oftentimes, I get frustrated, but that just makes the successes mean so much more.

what medium can i embroider on?

I embroider on white cotton squares, but I would recommend something more durable such as toweling. Sometimes, cotton will start to shed and tear, but toweling is strong and can bring some style to your piece.

how thick should my embroidery floss be?

To begin, I recommend using three out of six strands of your embroidery floss. Once you get more skilled at embroidering, you may move onto smaller sets of strands. I like using two strands for a satin stitch and three for a backstitch.

can you teach me?

Maybe. I can give you some guidance on what stitches to do for your design and how to plan your piece out. Yet, if you’re looking for a tutorial or lesson, refer to question one.
